# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Qualified, professional and institutional investors across Europe (plus retail
prospects who must register to unlock data): wealth managers, family offices,
and private investors evaluating Framont's product shelf. They arrive
deliberately, usually on desktop, expecting the discretion and composure of a
private bank. The job to be done: understand the four product lines (Funds,
AMC, ETI, VC deals), judge whether Framont is credible, and request access or
subscribe via InvestGlass onboarding.

## Product Purpose

Framont Access is the public storefront of the Framont Group investment
platform. It presents institutional-grade products, gates sensitive data
(ISINs, minimum subscriptions, documents) behind free registration, and routes
subscription requests into secure onboarding. Success = a visitor trusts the
brand enough to hand over their identity, and a member finds a product and
starts a subscription.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, composed, discreetly confident. "Private bank, not fintech
app." The interface should evoke calm authority and quiet exclusivity, the
feeling of being let into a room most people don't see. Bilingual (EN/IT),
Malta-licensed, compliance-forward: the legal fine print is part of the
credibility, not an embarrassment.

## Anti-references

- Retail trading apps (Robinhood-style gamification, confetti, green candles).
- Crypto-landing maximalism: neon gradients, 3D coins, hype copy.
- Generic SaaS landing grammar: gradient hero text, emoji bullets, hero-metric
  stat rows, cookie-cutter card grids.
- Stock-photo handshakes and skyline banks; imagery must feel graded and
  cinematic, never clip-art corporate.

## Design Principles

1. **The data is the luxury.** Locked ISINs and minimums are the product tease;
   gate elegantly (blur, lock marks), never shout.
2. **Motion like a title sequence, not a dashboard.** Slow, cinematic,
   exponential ease-out; one orchestrated moment per view beats scattered
   effects. Every animation has a reduced-motion fallback.
3. **Navy is the room, orange is the signature.** Deep navy (#001F42/#002E5F)
   carries atmosphere; Framont orange (#FF8200) appears only where attention or
   action lives.
4. **Serif speaks, sans works.** Playfair Display for statements and titles
   (committed brand identity), Work Sans for UI, labels, and data.
5. **Compliance is part of the design.** Disclaimers, eligibility and MFSA
   language stay legible and composed; regulatory seriousness is a brand asset.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA intent: body text contrast ≥4.5:1, visible keyboard focus rings,
semantic controls. `prefers-reduced-motion` users get static or crossfade
alternatives for all scroll choreography and ambient video (poster images
instead of autoplaying loops). Bilingual EN/IT content must remain
fully functional in both languages.
